EVENT PLANNING 101

Where to Start? A Checklist for Your First Big Event

Published on September 13, 2025

Planning a major event can feel overwhelming. Whether it's a corporate conference, a wedding, or a milestone celebration, the key to success is a solid plan. Breaking down the process into manageable steps ensures that no detail is overlooked. This guide provides a foundational checklist to get you started on the right foot.

1. Define Your Vision & Goals

Before you book a venue or hire a vendor, you must define the "why" behind your event. What is its primary purpose? Is it to celebrate, to educate, to launch a product, or to network? Clearly defining your objectives will guide every subsequent decision, from the guest list to the decor. Write down three key goals for your event before moving on.

2. Establish Your Budget

Your budget is the most critical tool in your planning arsenal. It dictates the scale of your event, the vendors you can hire, and the experience you can provide. Create a detailed spreadsheet and allocate funds to major categories: venue, catering, entertainment, marketing, and a contingency fund (always have a 10-15% buffer for unexpected costs).
